Muir's Life

  • 1838 - Born in Dunbar, Scotland
  • 1849 - Family emigrated to the United States, settling on a farm in Wisconsin
  • 1860 - Went to college at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where he studied botany and geology
  • 1867 - After being nearly blinded from an accident, he embarked on a 1,000 mile walk from Indiana to the Gulf of Mexico
  • 1868 - He first arrived in Yosemite Valley, which became a central focus of his conservation efforts
  • 1871 - Discovered that glaciers were responsible for creating Yosemite Valley
  • 1880 - Married Louisa Strentzel and settled on her family's fruit ranch in Martinez, California
  • 1890 - Yosemite National Park is established thanks to his efforts to preserve wilderness
  • 1892 - Founded the Sierra Club, an environmental organization dedicated to protecting wilderness areas
  • 1903 - Went camping in Yosemite with Theodore Roosevelt, which influenced him to establish additional national parks, monuments, and forests
  • 1914 - Dies in Los Angeles, California at 76

If you'd like to learn more about John Muir and some of his other accomplishments, visit his Wikipedia page.
